A proposal to start publishing courses with the Globant brand, to fulfill internal needs and for detecting specially talented students and trying to recruit them.

It is said that this is the shape of the future highschool education.

MOOC

This section is brief information about the "MOOC" concept and the main players.

TechCrunch on MOOCs as of Nov 2012:

Of late, one of the hottest subjects in education has been MOOCs, otherwise known as Massively Open Online Courses. There has been a parade of new MOOC platforms over the last six months, with the most recognizable names now being Coursera, Udacity and EdX, the collaboration between Harvard, MIT and UC Berkeley. MOOCs have been pegged by some as representing the future of the educational system, democratizing education, bringing quality learning content to people of any age, in any corner of the world for a small fee — if not for free.

Several companies, most notably coursera, are offering these free courses as an attempt to detect and hire new talent.

For us there would be additional advantages, like implement internal education, getting positioned, get other companies to know Globant, earn prestige, rub our shouders with globally prestigious universities, step into the future of highschool education.

MOOC at Globant

I'm proposing that Globant jumped into the MOOC trend, by offering a world-class battery of courses, initially about what we do best: managing IT development projects.

This is the single subject that would have the most significant impact for the company, effective over all the projects, aligning all the managers behind a tested set of best practices and ideas.

MOOCs usually span 4 to 10 weeks, I think of a set (TBD) of correlated brief courses, each one much smaller than regular MOOCs, designed so that busy people can take them in spare time, but following a predefined structured path, like the prerequisites for enrolling in university courses.

Proposed course subjects

Each subject would lead to a single course or a set of courses, depending on its width.

Software requirements
Understanding how to understand the project goal, in detail.
Use cases
Characteristics of effective UCs, as in Alistair Cockburn's book.
User stories
What are USs and how they should be written.
The QA 150 questions, in detail
Detailed explanation and justification of each one, with examples and related real-life testimonials of PMs.
Agile process
Basic knowledge, and what the PM should check during the meetings in order for the team to stay on track and sustain speed.

Course sources

There are two facets to make a course: the content and the form.

Content sources

We can summon the most recognized teachers and ask them to participate providing class material that they have already been using for years.

Ideally those teachers would be somehow "sponsored" by the University they work for. The University would provide the academic prestige, and Globant the technical framework.

The teachers should be enticed to participate in order to gain fame and future credibility. Actually, their gain could be important, like to be invited to dictate classes in foreign countries.

The courses will be advertised as "course by professor X, University Y, powered by Globant".

Also, we should ask outstanding Globant managers to share their wisdom, in order to have at least par ot it preserved in case they leave the Company or change their position.

Form shaping

The ability to shape a course so that it's solid but amenable, conveying the knowledge advertised by the title, packaged into the state-of-the-art MOOC form, is something we need to develop and provide as a service to those approaching to publish through our platform.

We must develop best practices and make them available, by sure as a MOOC building course.

It is important for the Globant-branded courses to be just perfect because the courses quality will be evaluated by those who see it as a measure for the quality of our products.

Software platform

There are two open source MOOC system: edX's and one developed in Spain. Both look good.

We have to choose one and run it in one of our servers.

The platform has to be intervened in order for it to fulfill our purposes:

  • deliver both internal and public courses keeping the domains separated, and
  • keep a detailed track of each student's performance.

The platform should run with a "powered by Globant" branding, independent of the course source.

One goal can be to make Globant the leading MOOC platform operator of all Latin America.

This would allow us (with the right agreements) to screen the participants of many MOOCs developed by somebody anywhere, mostly universities, and detect fresh outstanding talent.

Balance

Pros:

  • Detect exceptional talent
  • Provide and consume internal education
  • Get in contact and be associated with, other prominent MOOC players
  • Be seen as a CSR company
  • Get other companies to know Globant
  • Anchor (at least part of) the gained experience inside the company
  • Communicate knowledge accross the company branches

Cons:

  • Spend money (that might be regarded as charity donations)
  • We haven't started yet.
